Influence Games: Fake Endorsements & Fake Reviews
Fake Endorsements & Fake Reviews
Fake reviews and endorsements are tactics companies use to make a product or service appear more popular or trustworthy than it really is. Some businesses pay people to leave positive reviews, even if they have never used the product. Others may falsely claim that a celebrity supports their product, using the celebrity's name without permission or suggesting an endorsement that never happened. Social media influencers can also mislead consumers when they promote products without clearly disclosing sponsorships or paid partnerships. These practices give shoppers a false sense of trust, making it harder to know which products are genuinely good and which are being marketed through deception.
Common Tricks in Fake Celebrity Endorsements
  • AI Deepfakes: Scammers use AI to create fake videos of celebrities promoting products they've never used. The technology is getting so good these videos look completely real.
  • Photo Editing: They take real celebrity photos and digitally add products or put the star's face on fake ads. Basic cut-and-paste trickery that looks convincing.
  • Fake Social Media: Fraudsters create accounts that look almost identical to real celebrity profiles, with slightly different usernames or old photos. These fake accounts then post product endorsements.
  • Phony News Sites: Scammers build websites that look like real news outlets and publish fake articles about celebrity endorsements. They often use names similar to actual news organizations.
Ways to Stay Safe from Fake Endorsements and Reviews:
  • Verify endorsements through official sources and trusted channels.
  • Be skeptical of overly positive or generic reviews.
  • Check for transparency disclosures like #ad or #sponsored tags.
  • Use multiple review platforms for a balanced perspective.
  • Avoid purchasing from sellers that suppress negative reviews.
  • Report suspicious endorsements or reviews to relevant authorities.
