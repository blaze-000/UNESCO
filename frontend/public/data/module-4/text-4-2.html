RED FLAGS: Quick Signs of a Deceptive Ad
Why it works: Most tricky ads hinge on three things—what they claim, what backs it up, and what they hide in the fine print. CED helps you test all three in under a minute.
1. Claim
  1. The exact promise: price (“70% OFF”), performance (“best battery life”), availability (“only 3 left”), comparison (“better than Brand X”).
  2. Red flags: “Up to…”, “As low as…”, “#1”, “Best in class”, “Free”.
2. Evidence
  1. Look for sources: third-party tests, certified labs, dated studies, clear methods (sample size, conditions).
  2. Red flags: testimonials only, “study on file” with no link, vague badges, tiny footnotes, cherry-picked results.
3. Disclosure
  1. Scan for limits, fees, and conditions: auto-renewals, shipping/handling, restocking fees, region limits, “from” prices.
  2. Red flags: asterisks everywhere, unreadable fine print, counters that always reset, “free trial” that converts quietly.
